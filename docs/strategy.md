# Strategy

The strategic read for TCSW 2026, distilled from 12 years of pattern data.

## SWOT

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|
| 12-year brand; largest startup week in the Midwest | Attendance dropped 17K–19K to 1K (format uncertainty) |
| Fortune 500 corridor sponsor pool | Key website pages (sponsors, FAQ) return 404 |
| Multi-stakeholder ecosystem | Lean single-operator capacity (BETA) |
| MN Cup anchor + government support (DEED) | Lost tech.mn media channel |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|---|---|
| AI Innovation Day aligns with peak demand | National startup weeks competing harder |
| Corporate re-engagement (Target, 3M, Microsoft, AWS) | Volatile VC market pressures sponsor budgets |
| New Investor Day as a deal-making hub | Format confusion from the attendance drop |
| Six campuses expand touchpoints | Leadership-transition continuity risk |

## Positioning line

> Twin Cities Startup Week is the only event in the Midwest where Fortune 500 innovators, venture capital firms, and first-time founders converge on the same stage, in the same city, for five consecutive days.
