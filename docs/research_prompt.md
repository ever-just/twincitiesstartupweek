# Twin Cities Startup Week - Research Prompt

## Master Research Prompt

You are tasked with conducting comprehensive research on **Twin Cities Startup Week** to compile all available historical data, sponsor information, event details, and related documentation.

### Research Scope

Gather **ALL** available data on Twin Cities Startup Week including:

1. **Historical Event Data** (all years available)
   - Event dates, times, and venues
   - Complete session schedules and topics
   - Keynote speakers and panelists
   - Attendance numbers and demographics
   - Event themes and key announcements

2. **Sponsor Information** (complete history)
   - All sponsors by year (2015-present)
   - Sponsorship tiers and levels
   - Sponsor company profiles and industries
   - Logo and marketing materials
   - Sponsorship amounts (if public)

3. **Speaker & Session Data**
   - Speaker names, titles, companies
   - Session descriptions and outcomes
   - Video recordings (if available)
   - Speaker bios and social profiles

4. **Media & Press Coverage**
   - News articles mentioning the event
   - Press releases and announcements
   - Podcast mentions and interviews
   - Social media coverage and hashtags
   - Video content and recordings

5. **Community & Attendee Information**
   - Attendee testimonials and reviews
   - Investor participation data
   - Startup participation records
   - University and educational institution involvement
   - Community impact metrics

6. **Official Documents**
   - Event programs and agendas
   - Sponsorship packages and media kits
   - Marketing materials and brochures
   - Volunteer information
   - Registration data (if accessible)

### Research Methods

**Primary Sources:**
- Official website: twincitiestartupweek.com
- Social media: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook
- Wayback Machine: https://web.archive.org/web/*/twincitiestartupweek.com/*
- Local news outlets: Star Tribune, Pioneer Press, local Minneapolis/St. Paul media

**Secondary Sources:**
- National tech publications: TechCrunch, VentureBeat, Forbes
- Industry databases: Crunchbase, LinkedIn, PitchBook
- Event platforms: EventBrite, Meetup, Lunchclub
- Community forums: Reddit, local startup communities

**Search Strategies:**
- Google: `"Twin Cities Startup Week" [year/sponsor/speaker]`
- LinkedIn: Company page, posts, event pages
- Twitter: Hashtags #TCSW #TwinCitiesStartupWeek
- News archives: Google News, local news sites
- Archive.org: Historical website snapshots

### Data Organization

Store findings in the following structure:

```
research/
├── data_collection/
│   ├── official_website/          # Website content and structure
│   ├── historical_snapshots/      # Wayback Machine archives
│   ├── sponsors/                  # Sponsor lists and profiles
│   ├── speakers_and_sessions/     # Speaker bios and session info
│   ├── media_coverage/            # News articles and press releases
│   ├── social_media/              # Posts, hashtags, engagement data
│   └── documents/                 # Programs, materials, PDFs
├── analysis/                      # Processed insights and reports
└── raw_data/                      # Structured data (CSV, JSON)
```

### Deliverables

For each research area, provide:

1. **Comprehensive List**: All items found with sources
2. **Structured Data**: CSV or JSON format where applicable
3. **Summary Report**: Key findings and insights
4. **Source Documentation**: URLs, access dates, and notes
5. **Gaps Analysis**: What data is missing or unavailable

### Quality Standards

- ✓ Include source URLs for all information
- ✓ Note access dates for all data collected
- ✓ Verify information across multiple sources when possible
- ✓ Preserve original documents and links
- ✓ Organize chronologically by year when applicable
- ✓ Use consistent naming and formatting
