# Twin Cities Startup Week - Research Plan

## Objective
Compile all available data on Twin Cities Startup Week including historical information, past sponsors, events, attendees, and related documentation.

---

## Research Strategy

### 1. Official Website & Social Media
- **Primary Source**: twincitiestartupweek.com (main website)
- **Social Media Channels**:
  - LinkedIn (company page, posts, announcements)
  - Twitter/X (@tcsw or similar handles)
  - Instagram (event photos, announcements)
  - Facebook (event pages, community posts)
- **Action**: Archive all pages, download event descriptions, speaker bios, sponsor lists

### 2. Historical Event Data
- **Past Years**: Research 2015-2025 events (or whenever TCSW started)
- **For Each Year Collect**:
  - Event dates and schedule
  - Keynote speakers and session topics
  - Attendance numbers
  - Venue locations
  - Event themes
  - Press releases and announcements
- **Sources**:
  - Wayback Machine (archive.org) for historical website snapshots
  - LinkedIn event pages (archived)
  - EventBrite listings (if applicable)
  - Local news archives (Minneapolis/St. Paul media)

### 3. Sponsor Research
- **Current Sponsors**: Identify all 2024-2025 sponsors
- **Historical Sponsors**: Track sponsor evolution over years
- **For Each Sponsor Collect**:
  - Company name and industry
  - Sponsorship tier/level
  - Years of sponsorship
  - Sponsorship amount (if public)
  - Logo and company info
- **Sources**:
  - Official website sponsor pages
  - Past event programs and materials
  - LinkedIn company pages
  - Press releases mentioning sponsors

### 4. Media & Press Coverage
- **News Articles**:
  - Local Minneapolis/St. Paul news outlets (Star Tribune, Pioneer Press, etc.)
  - National startup/tech publications (TechCrunch, VentureBeat, etc.)
  - Industry-specific publications
- **Podcasts & Videos**:
  - YouTube recordings of sessions
  - Podcast mentions or interviews
  - Conference recordings
- **Action**: Create a media database with publication, date, URL, and summary

### 5. Community & Attendee Data
- **Attendee Information**:
  - Registration data (if accessible)
  - Attendee testimonials and reviews
  - LinkedIn posts from attendees
  - Community discussions (Reddit, local forums)
- **Networking Data**:
  - Investor participation
  - Startup participation
  - University/educational institution involvement

### 6. Document Collection
- **Official Materials**:
  - Event programs and agendas
  - Speaker bios and presentations
  - Sponsorship packages and media kits
  - Marketing materials and brochures
  - Volunteer information
- **Action**: Download and organize by year

### 7. Competitive & Regional Context
- **Similar Events**:
  - Other startup weeks in US (Austin, Denver, etc.)
  - Regional tech events in Minnesota
- **Economic Data**:
  - Minnesota startup ecosystem reports
  - Twin Cities tech industry growth metrics
  - Venture capital activity in region

---

## Data Collection Tools & Methods

### Search Strategies
1. **Google Search Operators**:
   - `site:twincitiestartupweek.com` (on-site search)
   - `"Twin Cities Startup Week" 2024` (year-specific)
   - `"Twin Cities Startup Week" sponsor` (sponsor-specific)
   - `"Twin Cities Startup Week" speaker` (speaker-specific)

2. **Archive & Wayback Machine**:
   - https://web.archive.org/web/*/twincitiestartupweek.com/*
   - Search for snapshots from each year

3. **Social Media Search**:
   - LinkedIn: Search company name, use filters by date
   - Twitter: Search hashtags (#TCSW, #TwinCitiesStartupWeek)
   - Instagram: Search hashtags and location tags

4. **News Aggregators**:
   - Google News Archive
   - NewsGuard or similar news search engines
   - Local Minnesota news outlets directly

5. **Database & Directory Search**:
   - Crunchbase (startup and investor data)
   - LinkedIn (company and person profiles)
   - PitchBook (investor and funding data)

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Attendance**: Year-over-year growth
- **Sponsors**: Number, diversity, tier levels
- **Sessions**: Count, topics, speaker diversity
- **Geographic Reach**: Where attendees/sponsors come from
- **Media Coverage**: Articles, reach, sentiment
- **Investor Participation**: Number, funding activity
- **Startup Participation**: Number of startups, industries represented

---

## Data Storage & Organization

- **Format**: Mix of markdown docs, CSV spreadsheets, and JSON for structured data
- **Backup**: Keep original sources and links for verification
- **Citations**: Always include source URL and access date
- **Versioning**: Date all snapshots and archives

---

## Potential Challenges & Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Outdated website links | Use Wayback Machine for historical access |
| Incomplete sponsor data | Cross-reference with news articles and LinkedIn |
| Missing attendance numbers | Estimate from venue capacity and media reports |
| Paywalled articles | Use library access, Google Scholar, or contact authors |
| Social media deletions | Use archive.org and social media archive tools |
