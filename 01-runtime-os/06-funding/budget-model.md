## 1. Purpose of this model

This budget model exists to answer one question:

> **What funding structure makes TCSW 2026 believable, fundable, and deliverable without putting BETA back in harm's way or asking SMS to absorb unpaid production risk?**

The model should help BETA and SMS define:

- minimum viable cash need;
- target cash need;
- stretch cash need;
- in-kind value requirements;
- SMS compensation;
- contractor budget;
- venue decision thresholds;
- sponsor targets;
- go / no-go checkpoints;
- fallback scope if funding comes in below target.

---

## 2. Core budget thesis

The budget is not just an expense list.

It is a trust instrument.

A credible budget allows Angela to fundraise with confidence, allows Linda / the board to understand risk, allows SMS to staff the work responsibly, and prevents TCSW 2026 from repeating a pattern where ambition outruns operational capacity.

> **The budget must protect BETA, compensate SMS, discipline sponsor promises, and make the public experience deliverable.**

---

## 3. Budget principles

### 3.1 BETA protection first

BETA should not absorb another financially harmful year.

The budget must show what cash must be secured before major commitments are made.

### 3.2 SMS must be paid for production responsibility

SMS cannot carry end-to-end production, programming, operations, creative, and product work through unpaid enthusiasm.

The budget must include SMS compensation and contractor support as core infrastructure, not optional extras.

### 3.3 In-kind is real value, but not cash

In-kind venue, platform, marketing, media, food, AV, or community support reduces cost but does not replace cash needed for labor, coordination, contingency, and production risk.

In-kind must be tracked separately from cash.

### 3.4 No sponsor promise without budget support

Sponsor deliverables must be tied to available budget, venue capacity, staffing, and operational reality.

### 3.5 Use funding thresholds to control scope

TCSW 2026 should have multiple versions:

- minimum viable version;
- target version;
- stretch version;
- fallback / reduced-scope version;
- no-go or pause thresholds for risky commitments.

### 3.6 Contingency is not optional

The shorter the timeline, the more important contingency becomes.

A contingency line protects BETA and SMS from unknowns.

## 4. Budget levels

## 4.1 Minimum viable budget

### Definition

The minimum viable budget is the amount required to deliver TCSW 2026 safely, credibly, and simply without harming BETA.

This version prioritizes trust restoration over scale.

### Minimum viable version should include

- paid SMS production leadership;
- essential contractor support;
- confirmed venue model, ideally low-cost or in-kind;
- basic programming architecture;
- essential marketing / landing page support;
- basic attendee communications;
- basic signage and wayfinding;
- basic hospitality;
- SMS app TCSW connection-layer MVP support;
- volunteer coordination support;
- contingency.

### Minimum viable version should not promise

- large attendance numbers;
- complex multi-site flow;
- expensive venue production;
- high-touch sponsor activations;
- large media / PR campaign;
- major app-scale claims;
- shuttle / transportation operations;
- anything requiring staffing that is not funded.

## 4.4 Fallback / reduced-scope budget

### Definition

The fallback budget is the version SMS and BETA can deliver if sponsorship comes in late or below expectations.

This version should be explicitly designed so BETA is not trapped into overcommitment.

### Fallback version may include

- fewer venues;
- fewer rooms;
- fewer official sessions;
- stronger reliance on partner-hosted programming;
- lighter hospitality;
- limited sponsor activations;
- reduced creative production;
- simpler app role;
- fewer paid contractors;
- more transparent attendee expectations.

### Fallback principle

> **Reduce scope before reducing trust.**

---

## 4.5 No-go / pause thresholds

Certain commitments should not be made until funding or in-kind support is secured.

Examples:

| Commitment | Required before committing |
|---|---|
| Paid venue deposit | Confirmed cash or written in-kind agreement |
| Paid contractors | SMS/BETA compensation and contractor budget approval |
| Sponsor-hosted activation | Venue space, staff capacity, and sponsor agreement |
| Public attendance claims | Registration funnel and marketing plan |
| App sponsor benefits | MVP readiness, privacy policy, user experience review |
| Multi-site programming | Venue map, staffing, wayfinding, and transportation reality |
| Hospitality promises | Food / beverage budget or in-kind partner |
| Media capture | Confirmed media budget or in-kind partner |

## 8. Funding thresholds

The funding plan should define thresholds for action.

| Threshold | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Threshold 0 — Discovery | No committed funding beyond existing resources | Do not make major commitments; build budget, venue pipeline, sponsor offer |
| Threshold 1 — Activation | Enough cash to pay SMS activation and begin formal production | SMS begins first 30-day sprint |
| Threshold 2 — Venue confidence | Enough cash / in-kind to secure venue model | Public campaign and sponsor promises become more concrete |
| Threshold 3 — Minimum viable event | Enough cash to deliver simple, credible TCSW | Confirm reduced-scope version if target funding lags |
| Threshold 4 — Target event | Enough cash to deliver professionally rebuilt TCSW | Execute target plan |
| Threshold 5 — Stretch event | Funding exceeds target with enough time to deploy well | Add stretch features without creating chaos |

## 12. Decision checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1 — Partnership authorization

BETA authorizes SMS to create final scope, budget, and first 30-day sprint.

### Checkpoint 2 — Activation funding

BETA and/or sponsors commit enough to pay SMS activation and begin real production.

### Checkpoint 3 — Venue commitment

Venue strategy is selected and funded / secured in-kind.

### Checkpoint 4 — Public campaign launch

Only launch public promises that the budget and venue model can support.

### Checkpoint 5 — Contractor hiring

Hire contractors only once budget is approved.

### Checkpoint 6 — Sponsor deliverable lock

Sponsor benefits are locked only after venue, staffing, and programming capacity are validated.

## 13. Budget risk register

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Sponsorship comes in late | SMS and BETA carry unpaid planning burden | Activation fee and funding checkpoints |
| Venue costs exceed expectation | Budget consumed before production is staffed | Prioritize in-kind venue and multiple options |
| Sponsor promises outrun budget | Trust breaks again | Feasibility checklist before promising |
| SMS underpaid | Production quality suffers; team burns out | Production compensation as core budget category |
| BETA fronts too much | Nonprofit risk repeats | Define no-go thresholds and board approval requirements |
| App overpromised | Product trust risk | Tie app sponsor benefits to MVP readiness |
| Contractor budget missing | Rachel cannot execute safely | Contractor budget required in minimum / target model |
| No contingency | Small problems become financial emergencies | 10%–15% placeholder contingency |
