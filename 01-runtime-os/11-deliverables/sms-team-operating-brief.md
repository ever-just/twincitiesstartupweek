# SMS Team Operating Brief

**Document type:** Internal operating brief  
**Audience:** SMS core team  
**Prepared by:** SMS Runtime OS  
**Date:** 2026-06-06  
**Event:** Twin Cities Startup Week 2026  
**Event dates:** September 14–18, 2026

---

## 1. The current posture

SMS has been invited into a serious partnership conversation with BETA around TCSW 2026.

This is not yet a finalized agreement.

We are preparing to become the **production and community experience engine** for TCSW 2026, but we do not yet have final authority, budget, compensation, or signed scope.

Our posture:

> **Prepare the system. Do not overcommit the team before authority and funding are clear.**

---

## 2. What we believe is true

TCSW 2026 is not simply a week of events.

It is the rebuild of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

BETA has the institutional trust and historical equity. Angela is carrying too much operational weight. SMS may be the right production partner because our work has always been about turning gatherings into human connection.

The working thesis:

> **The Great Reunion — the act of coming together again.**

This means the ecosystem coming together again: founders, funders, universities, students, operators, technologists, institutions, sponsors, and new voices.

---

## 3. What is not yet true

Do not act as if these are already settled:

- SMS does not yet have a signed agreement.
- SMS does not yet have confirmed budget.
- SMS does not yet have full decision rights.
- SMS does not yet have confirmed venues.
- SMS does not yet have final sponsor authority.
- SMS app has not yet been publicly launched or approved as a TCSW layer.
- SMS team capacity has not yet been formally budgeted.
- Contractor support is not yet funded.

This means all work before approval should be framed as preparation, discovery, and proposal-building.

---

## 4. The opportunity

If approved, SMS would serve as:

> **End-to-End Production & Community Experience Partner for TCSW 2026.**

This would mean SMS leads or supports:

- venue sourcing and location architecture;
- programming architecture;
- run-of-show planning;
- host, speaker, attendee, sponsor, and volunteer experience;
- brand, marketing, landing page, maps, and campaign assets;
- sponsor-support materials and feasibility review;
- SMS app connection layer;
- lead-up engagement;
- trackers, decision logs, risks, and operating cadence;
- first 30-day sprint.

The division we are proposing:

> **BETA holds the institutional trust. Angela closes and stewards relationships. SMS produces the coherent public experience.**

## 7. What not to do yet

Do not:

- announce SMS's role publicly;
- contact sponsors as if SMS is already authorized;
- make venue promises;
- promise app features to BETA or sponsors;
- recruit contractors without budget clarity;
- begin a full creative build before the decision frame is approved;
- assume Rachel can carry all production details alone;
- treat the Monday meeting as final approval;
- tell anyone SMS owns or controls TCSW.

Use this language internally:

> **We are preparing to carry the production layer if authorized.**
