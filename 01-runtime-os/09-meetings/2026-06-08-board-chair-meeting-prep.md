Angela has invited SMS into her recurring meeting with board chair **Linda Finley** because, if the conversation goes well, it could accelerate board approval for the proposed:

> **SMS x BETA: End-to-End Production & Community Experience Partnership for TCSW 2026**

3. why TCSW 2026 is a high-stakes rebuild moment;

## 5. What SMS must prove

### 5.1 SMS understands BETA's capacity problem

Angela is holding fundraising, marketing, board communication, venue strategy, sponsor repair, programming structure, and nonprofit stewardship while also protecting BETA's core work.

The accelerator and BETA's ongoing operations cannot be sacrificed to produce TCSW.

SMS must show that it understands Angela's immediate operational pain:

- marketing and fundraising need her attention;
- logistics are consuming her attention;
- Dylan cannot absorb the event-production burden;
- the board needs clearer direction before it can help;
- venues, rooms, spreadsheets, days, speakers, timeframes, and granular details need a dedicated owner.

### 5.2 SMS understands TCSW's trust problem

The issue is not just that TCSW needs to happen.

The issue is that TCSW must happen in a way that raises trust.

Past years created some mismatch between what was promised and what was delivered. Angela is entering 2026 with the burden of repair.

SMS must show that it understands:

- overpromising is dangerous;
- sponsor trust has to be restored;
- attendee trust has to be rebuilt;
- partner trust has to be protected;
- BETA cannot afford another year that taxes the organization without restoring value.

### 5.3 SMS understands the funding problem

Funding is the most urgent constraint, but funding is not just a sales problem.

Funding depends on belief.

Sponsors need to see that TCSW 2026 is:

- professionally held;
- operationally credible;
- publicly shareable;
- culturally relevant;
- visible;
- timely;
- worth attaching their name to.

SMS's job is to help create the thing Angela can confidently fundraise around.

### 5.4 SMS understands the nonprofit / for-profit sensitivity

BETA has real nonprofit and community equity.

SMS is a for-profit organization.

Therefore, the partnership must be structured so that:

- BETA's brand and 14-year ecosystem equity are respected;
- SMS is not perceived as taking over or extracting value;
- SMS is paid for real labor and production responsibility;
- BETA is protected from financial harm;
- responsibilities, authority, credit, and risk are balanced in writing.

### 5.5 SMS understands the location problem

Location architecture is the first operational blocker.

Until the venue model is clear, the team cannot responsibly finalize:

- sponsor promises;
- programming capacity;
- room counts;
- daily flow;
- attendee experience;
- sponsor visibility;
- host expectations;
- app-enabled meetup design.

The first execution priority is to make the physical system legible.

## 7. Why end-to-end matters

The reason SMS is proposing end-to-end responsibility is not scope inflation.

It is risk control.

TCSW's core risk is fragmentation:

- fragmented venues;
- fragmented programming;
- fragmented marketing;
- fragmented sponsor promises;
- fragmented attendee experience;
- fragmented ownership;
- fragmented communication.

Fragmentation is how trust breaks.

Therefore, if SMS is expected to make the public experience coherent, SMS must have enough authority to connect the touchpoints.

> **End-to-end responsibility requires end-to-end authority.**
