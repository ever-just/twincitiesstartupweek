# Proposal: TCSW 2026 – "The Reunion"

## 1. Theme & Vision: "The Reunion"
Twin Cities Startup Week (TCSW) 2026 is themed **"The Reunion"**—a homecoming for Minnesota's innovation ecosystem. This initiative is a strategic effort to rebuild trust and restore the legacy of TCSW through a focused, 100-day operational recovery. We are moving beyond event planning into a phase of ecosystem restoration, bringing together the founders, investors, and corporate partners who built this community.

## 2. The Structural Shift: Attention Routing & Calendar Leverage
We are transitioning from a capital-heavy event planning model to a high-margin **attention routing** engine. By "owning the map" through Calendar Leverage, we shift the focus from hosting isolated events to directing the flow of the entire ecosystem. We aren't just planning a week; we are managing the distribution of attention across the city, turning the region into a distributed campus.

## 3. The Three-Product Engine
Our revenue model is built on three distinct, high-impact products:
1.  **Status Quo Presenting Sponsors (The 'Why'):** Anchor capital from large corporate and presenting partners. These sponsors provide the stability and foundational support that makes the Reunion possible.
2.  **Ticket Sales (The 'Energy'):** Direct revenue from attendee participation. This represents the community’s "skin in the game" and serves as a primary metric for the week's raw energy and reach.
3.  **The Open Doors Calendar (The 'Toll Booth'):** Operating on Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday, this is a premium marketplace where companies pay to "buy traffic and attention" by opening their offices to the TCSW crowd. This turns private spaces into public activations, with TCSW acting as the routing layer.

## 4. The 5-Tranche Waterfall
To ensure professional execution and financial sustainability, revenue will be distributed according to the following waterfall:
*   **Tranche 1: SMS Engine Fee ($30,000 Non-Refundable):**
    *   **$25,000 Capacity Reservation:** Secures the "5x5" senior recovery team (Liban, Matthew, Rachel, Abhishek, and Weldon) at $5,000 each.
    *   **$5,000 Infrastructure:** Covers Odoo CRM setup, automation, and operational launch costs.
*   **Tranche 2: Beta Recoupment:** 100% of revenue to Beta until all verified internal costs are recouped.
*   **Tranche 3: Growth Split:** Net profit split at 80/20 (SMS/Beta).
*   **Tranche 4: Scaling Split:** Sliding scale adjustment toward a 50/50 split as revenue milestones are met.
*   **Tranche 5: Legacy/Treasury:** 20/80 (SMS/Beta) split directed toward the nonprofit treasury to ensure long-term ecosystem health.

## 5. Governance & Data
*   **Brand Ownership:** Beta remains the sole owner of the Twin Cities Startup Week brand.
*   **Data Control:** All CRM and attendee data is hosted on Beta’s Odoo instance; Beta maintains full ownership of all ecosystem data generated.
*   **SMS Execution:** SMS provides professional execution and operational separation to ensure a high-velocity recovery.
*   **No-Poach Policy:** SMS commits to a strict 24-month no-poach policy regarding legacy TCSW/Beta sponsors and partners.
