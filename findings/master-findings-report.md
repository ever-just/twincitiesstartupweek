## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Twin Cities Startup Week (TCSW) is Minnesota's premier startup festival, run since 2014 by **The Beta Group (BETA)**, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. What began as 15 tech events with a few thousand attendees has grown into a week-long, multi-city convergence of entrepreneurs, investors, corporations, and community members. At its peak, TCSW drew **~17,000–19,000 attendees** across **200+ events**. The 2025 edition marked a pivot — a leaner, more founder-focused format co-hosted with **Communiful**, drawing 1,000+ directly engaged participants. TCSW 2026 is scheduled for **September 14–18** across six campuses.

## SECTION 2: HISTORICAL GROWTH

### Attendance Arc
- 2014: Few thousand (15 events, tech-only)
- 2018: 12,000+ (200+ events, 5th year)
- 2019: 17,000+ (200+ events, 6th year — first Fortune 500 title sponsors)
- 2020: ~19,000 (VIRTUAL — COVID, 3-week virtual format)
- 2021: ~17,000 (hybrid — 200 events, 80 virtual / 40 hybrid / 70 in-person)
- 2025: 1,000+ (new lean, founder-focused format co-hosted with Communiful)

### Format Evolution
1. **2014–2017:** Single-week, primarily tech, growing venue count
2. **2018:** Major expansion — added Food|Ag Ideas Week, BITCon, MANOVA Summit; first mobile app; Lyft bus
3. **2019:** Fortune 500 title sponsorship (Target/Cargill/3M); geographic clustering; first weekend-spanning edition
4. **2020:** Fully virtual (COVID); Hopin platform; 3 weeks to avoid digital fatigue
5. **2021:** Hybrid return
6. **2022–2023:** Full in-person return; Luminary Arts Center as main venue; Optum Cup naming rights
7. **2025:** Reformatted — leaner, deeper focus; Communiful co-host; removed headline attendance metric
8. **2026:** 6 campuses; themed days (Industry, AI Innovation, Investor, Give Back)
