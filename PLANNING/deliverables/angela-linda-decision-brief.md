# Angela / Linda Decision Brief

**Document type:** Decision-facing brief  
**Audience:** Angela Eifert + Linda Finley  
**Prepared by:** Strangers Meeting Strangers (SMS)  
**Date:** 2026-06-06  
**Event:** Twin Cities Startup Week 2026  
**Event dates:** September 14–18, 2026

---

## 1. Read this first

This brief is not a programming brainstorm.

It is a decision brief about the operating structure required to make TCSW 2026 believable, fundable, and deliverable.

SMS is not proposing to replace BETA or take TCSW away from BETA.

SMS is proposing to become the **end-to-end production and community experience partner** so BETA can remain the institutional steward while Angela focuses on sponsor relationships, board alignment, and long-term trust restoration.

Core division:

> **BETA holds the institutional trust. Angela closes and stewards relationships. SMS produces the coherent public experience.**

---

## 2. What SMS understands about the challenge

From the Jun 3 conversation, SMS understands that the challenge is not simply "we need programming."

The challenge is an integrated system:

1. **BETA has real ecosystem equity, but limited operating capacity.**
2. **Angela is carrying too many functions at once:** fundraising, marketing, sponsor repair, venue strategy, board communication, partner conversations, and event structure.
3. **TCSW has trust to rebuild** after years of operational strain and mismatch between promise and delivery.
4. **Funding is urgent, but funding depends on belief.** Sponsors need to see that this year is credible, coherent, and professionally held.
5. **Venue architecture is the immediate blocker.** Until rooms, capacities, costs, and location flow are clear, sponsor and programming promises remain unstable.
6. **The event should be community-powered, but not chaotic.** A community-led model still needs an operating container.

The core risk is fragmentation: fragmented venues, fragmented programming, fragmented sponsor promises, fragmented marketing, fragmented ownership, and fragmented attendee experience.

Fragmentation is how trust breaks.

---

## 3. SMS's proposal

SMS proposes to serve as:

> **End-to-End Production & Community Experience Partner for TCSW 2026.**

This means SMS would carry the integrated production layer across:

- venue sourcing and location architecture;
- programming architecture;
- host, speaker, volunteer, sponsor, and attendee journey;
- run-of-show planning;
- brand, campaign, landing page, and marketing assets;
- sponsor-support materials and sponsor-promise feasibility review;
- SMS app community connection layer;
- operating cadence, trackers, decision logs, and risk management;
- first 30-day sprint to move TCSW from possibility to operating reality.

This is not scope expansion for its own sake.

It is risk control.

> **End-to-end responsibility requires end-to-end authority.**

If SMS is accountable for making the public experience coherent, SMS needs enough authority over the touchpoints that create that experience.

---

## 4. Why this year matters

TCSW 2026 should not be understood as simply another event cycle.

It is the rebuild of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

AI, talent, founders, universities, investors, corporate innovation, and community builders are converging. The ecosystem needs a trusted room where these groups can come together again.

SMS's working frame is:

> **The Great Reunion — the act of coming together again.**

This is not nostalgia.

It means BETA coming back into trust, sponsors coming back into relationship, founders and funders coming back into proximity, universities and students entering more visibly, and new voices joining the future of Minnesota's startup ecosystem.

---

## 5. How BETA is protected

This model is designed to protect BETA, not dilute it.

BETA remains responsible for:

- TCSW brand stewardship;
- nonprofit legitimacy;
- sponsor contract authority;
- board governance;
- mission alignment;
- founder ecosystem credibility;
- final approval of major institutional, financial, legal, and brand-risk decisions.

SMS should not make final sponsor commitments, sign venue contracts, approve legal commitments, or make public claims that create institutional risk for BETA without BETA approval.

Board-safe framing:

> **BETA remains the steward. SMS becomes the production and community experience engine.**

## 7. The SMS app, simply stated

The SMS app is not being positioned as a replacement for TCSW's official ticketing or schedule system.

It is the community connection layer.

It answers a common large-event problem:

> **I am here, surrounded by people, and I still do not know who to meet.**

For TCSW, the app can support small-group meetups, first-time attendee entry points, post-session continuation circles, founder/customer discovery conversations, student/founder gatherings, investor/operator meetups, and sponsor-supported connection moments.

The app should be promised only within product-readiness, privacy, and platform-boundary guardrails.

---

## 11. What SMS will not do

SMS will not:

- claim ownership of TCSW;
- replace BETA's institutional role;
- treat TCSW as primarily an app launchpad;
- make sponsor promises before venue, staffing, budget, and product realities are clear;
- rely on unpaid enthusiasm as the production model;
- overpromise attendance, investor access, app adoption, media reach, or sponsor value;
- put BETA back in harm's way.

---

## 12. Closing thesis

> **The question is not whether TCSW needs help. The question is what operating structure can make TCSW 2026 believable, fundable, and deliverable without putting BETA back in harm's way. SMS believes it has assembled the team to carry that production layer, if BETA gives us the structure to do it responsibly.**
