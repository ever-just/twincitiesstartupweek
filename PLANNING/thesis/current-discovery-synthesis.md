## 1. Core conclusion

SMS should propose itself to BETA as the **end-to-end production and community experience partner** for Twin Cities Startup Week 2026.

This is not because SMS wants broader control for its own sake. It is because the challenge itself is integrated:

- funding depends on perceived value;
- perceived value depends on public trust;
- public trust depends on every touchpoint feeling coherent;
- coherence requires integrated production across venues, programming, brand, marketing, registration, sponsor experience, attendee journey, app-enabled connection, and post-event continuity.

Fragmented ownership will recreate fragmented experience. Fragmented experience will recreate the trust problem TCSW is trying to repair.

Therefore, SMS must not merely "help with programming." SMS must carry the production and community experience layer end-to-end, with clear authority, budget structure, decision rights, compensation, and risk boundaries.

---

## 2. What Angela revealed about the real challenge

The Jun 3 meeting was not primarily about programming ideas. It revealed the operating conditions around TCSW 2026.

### BETA has real ecosystem equity but limited operating capacity

BETA still holds the institutional legitimacy of TCSW, but Angela is carrying too many functions at once: fundraising, marketing, board communication, venue coordination, sponsor repair, program design, partner conversations, and nonprofit stewardship.

Angela repeatedly named the need for **true event planner energy**: locations, spreadsheets, daily schedules, speakers, timeframes, rooms, venue details, and the granular work required to make the event real.

### TCSW has a trust problem

The event still has brand equity, but past years created disappointment, confusion, operational friction, and mismatch between promise and delivery. Angela framed this year as one where BETA must raise trust and over-deliver relative to the past.

This means the 2026 challenge is not simply execution. It is trust restoration.

### Funding is the immediate constraint, but funding is tied to belief

Angela needs sponsor dollars, but sponsor dollars will be easier to close if the event feels believable, coherent, timely, and professionally held.

The funding problem is therefore also a perception problem: sponsors must believe that TCSW 2026 is not a patched-together version of the past, but a credible rebuild of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

### The event should be community-powered, not overproduced

Angela does not want BETA to overmanage or overproduce the entire week. She wants to create the container where speakers, founders, funders, universities, startups, sponsors, students, and ecosystem partners can participate.

This creates the precise need for SMS: a group capable of designing the social and operational container so community participation becomes coherent rather than chaotic.

### Location architecture is the first operational blocker

The first-order execution problem is not yet speaker selection. It is location clarity.

Until venues, rooms, capacities, day-by-day location architecture, and proximity strategy are defined, sponsor promises and programming promises remain unstable.

## 4. The strategic thesis

TCSW 2026 is not simply a week of events.

It is the rebuild of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

AI, talent, founders, universities, investors, corporate innovation, and community builders are converging at the same time. If done right, TCSW can become the moment where Minnesota's innovation ecosystem sees itself again.

For that to happen, every touchpoint must increase perceived and actual value for:

- BETA;
- sponsors and funders;
- attendees;
- speakers and hosts;
- founders;
- investors;
- universities;
- volunteers;
- community partners;
- SMS;
- the broader Minnesota ecosystem.

The event must feel fundable before it is fully funded. It must feel trustworthy before people fully return. It must feel shareable before the week begins.

## 5. The emotional frame: The Great Re-Union

The central frame is:

> **The Great Reunion — the act of coming together again.**

This is not nostalgia. It is not an insider re-union for only those who were already in the room.

It means the ecosystem itself is coming together again:

- BETA coming back into public trust;
- sponsors coming back into relationship;
- founders coming back into proximity;
- universities and students coming into the ecosystem more visibly;
- investors, operators, technologists, and institutions reassembling;
- new voices entering the room as part of the future.

A new attendee can be part of the reunion because reunion is an act, not a guest list.

## 6. The funding thesis

The funding problem is not only scarcity. It is valuation.

TCSW has been undervalued because its true civic and ecosystem value has not been made visible enough.

Sponsors do not fund an under-resourced event that needs help. They fund:

- momentum;
- credibility;
- access;
- cultural relevance;
- visibility;
- timing;
- association with a comeback;
- a public role in rebuilding something that matters.

The sponsor ask should therefore not be:

> "Please sponsor TCSW."

It should be:

> **"This is the year TCSW is being rebuilt as Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point. Your organization should be visibly part of bringing the ecosystem together again."**

The sponsor product must shift from logo placement to civic role.

---

## 7. The SMS app thesis

The SMS app is not a side project.

It is the connection layer that makes The Great Reunion operational.

Large events often fail at the human level because attendees think:

> "I am here, surrounded by people, and I still do not know who to meet."

The SMS app should answer that problem by enabling discovery of small-group meetups inside and around TCSW:

- first-time attendee tables;
- post-session continuation circles;
- founder/customer discovery meetups;
- student/founder conversations;
- investor/operator coffee groups;
- industry-specific micro-gatherings;
- host-led small group moments before, during, and after the week.

The app should not be positioned as a replacement for the official ticketing or schedule system.

It should be positioned as:

> **The community connection layer that helps large gatherings feel human.**

## 10. What remains unanswered

These are the unresolved questions that must become operating documents:

### Partnership structure

- What is the exact BETA/SMS agreement?
- Is SMS a production partner, community experience partner, operating partner, or some combination?
- What language is board-safe and accurate?

### Decision rights

- Who has final say on venues?
- Who has final say on programming?
- Who approves sponsor promises?
- Who controls public campaign language?
- Who approves budget tradeoffs?
- Who owns app integration decisions?

### Compensation and risk

- What does SMS need to be paid immediately?
- What can be deferred, if anything?
- What is contingent on sponsorship?
- What risk is BETA carrying?
- What risk is SMS carrying?
- What happens if sponsorship lands late or below target?

### Budget architecture

- What is the minimum viable event budget?
- What is the target budget?
- What is the stretch budget?
- What budget supports SMS team compensation and contractors?
- What budget protects BETA from being harmed again?

### Sponsor product

- What are sponsors actually buying?
- What are the tiers?
- What is the founding-partner offer?
- What is the role-based language around The Great Reunion?
- What sponsor promises can be safely made without overpromising?

### Venue architecture

- What is the ideal location model?
- Which venues are plausible, affordable, and accessible?
- How concentrated does the week need to be?
- Which locations become anchors?
- What spaces allow sponsor and programming promises to be made confidently?
