## 1. Core team thesis

SMS is not entering TCSW as a loose volunteer group, a single founder idea, or a narrow programming helper.

SMS is entering as an assembled production organization with core leadership across:

- executive production;
- program production;
- operations;
- venue and stakeholder intelligence;
- creative direction;
- product and engineering;
- community experience design.

The team was assembled through more than four years of SMS organizing work and thousands of community interactions.

This matters because TCSW 2026 requires more than energy. It requires trusted operators who can carry ambiguity, build structure, move quickly, and protect the quality of the public experience.

## 2. Why this team matters for BETA

Angela's central need is capacity.

BETA holds institutional trust and ecosystem equity, but Angela and her small nonprofit staff cannot carry the full event-production burden while also managing BETA's core work and sponsor relationships.

SMS maps directly to the gaps Angela named:

| BETA / TCSW need | SMS capacity |
|---|---|
| Event planner energy | Rachel + production contractors |
| Venue sourcing and location architecture | Weldon + Liban + SMS operations |
| Sponsor and stakeholder intelligence | Weldon + Angela + BETA board + SMS network |
| Public perception reset | Matthieu + Liban |
| Campaign / landing page / marketing assets | Matthieu + Liban |
| Human connection layer | Liban + Rachel + SMS community experience |
| App-enabled engagement | Abhishek + product/design/engineering support |
| Runtime operating system | Liban + Weldon |
| Trust-preserving structure | SMS x BETA operating model |

The team gives Angela a credible answer to the question:

> **Who will hold the production layer so BETA can focus on stewardship and sponsor closing?**

## 3. Team roles

## 3.1 Liban — Executive Producer / Vision Holder

Function: hold the entire operating frame.

Responsibilities:

- SMS x BETA relationship stewardship;
- strategic thesis and narrative;
- TCSW Runtime OS design;
- internal team orchestration;
- public positioning and campaign logic;
- landing page direction;
- partnership proposal development;
- milestone and delivery discipline;
- cross-functional alignment across programming, operations, creative, product, and funding.

Operating question:

> **Does the whole system still hold together?**

## 3.2 Rachel — Program Director

Function: translate the TCSW thesis into a coherent program experience.

Rachel directly enabled Talk to Me Day, SMS's largest festival of talking, and helped bring together more than 1,000 people through an experience with many vendors, moving parts, social flow, public space, and production complexity.

For TCSW, Rachel should not be treated as the entire production department. She should lead a program production team.

Responsibilities:

- day architecture;
- host and speaker intake;
- session fit and quality standards;
- run-of-show planning;
- volunteer and staffing needs;
- room flow;
- transition logic;
- first-time attendee experience;
- program feasibility against venue realities;
- on-site production leadership.

Operating question:

> **Can the program be experienced clearly, humanely, and credibly by attendees, hosts, sponsors, and volunteers?**

Capacity guardrail:

> Rachel needs a team she can orchestrate. TCSW cannot rely on Rachel alone to activate every contractor, host, vendor, volunteer, and run-of-show detail.

## 3.3 Weldon — COO / Operations + Research + Sponsor Intelligence

Function: build the operating infrastructure and stakeholder intelligence needed to execute.

Weldon owns SMS operations and has already stood up the historical TCSW research repository, including public research across past years, funders, venues, locations, speakers, sponsors, and other stakeholder signals.

This research makes The Great Reunion actionable. It helps SMS identify:

- who funded TCSW before;
- who hosted it;
- who spoke;
- which venues appeared;
- which sponsors disappeared;
- which partners need to be reactivated;
- which relationships require trust repair;
- where sponsor and venue opportunities may exist.

Responsibilities:

- venue pipeline;
- venue sourcing and procurement support;
- sponsor / stakeholder intelligence;
- sponsor pipeline support;
- relationship mapping;
- operations trackers;
- TCSW historical memory layer;
- coordination with Angela / BETA as authorized;
- operational rhythm with Liban and Rachel.

Operating question:

> **What does the historical and operational data tell us to do next?**

## 3.4 Matthieu — Creative Director / Internal Design Agency

Function: make the value of TCSW visible, felt, and shareable.

Matthieu has shaped SMS's branding, taste, cultural feel, maps, flyers, marketing ads, and public festival assets across Talk to Me Day 1 and 2.

For TCSW, Matthieu's role is not decorative. The funding problem is partly a perception problem, and creative direction is how perceived value becomes visible.

Responsibilities:

- TCSW visual system support;
- brand rebuild support;
- marketing assets;
- landing page design support;
- sponsor-facing materials;
- host-facing materials;
- attendee maps;
- printed assets;
- signage and wayfinding system;
- social templates;
- visual storytelling;
- shareability of the public campaign.

Operating question:

> **Does this feel valuable enough for people to share, sponsor, attend, and trust?**

## 3.5 Abhishek — Product / Engineering Lead

Function: build and deploy the SMS app as the TCSW community connection layer.

Abhishek leads engineering for the SMS app, supported by a smaller team of designers and engineers.

The product is in MVP stage and has not yet launched publicly or been shown to Angela.

The target is for the product to launch in June and be refined, tested, and ready enough to support TCSW use cases by September.

For TCSW, the SMS app should not be positioned as a replacement for the official ticketing or schedule platform.

It should be positioned as:

> **The community connection layer that helps large gatherings feel human.**

Responsibilities:

- TCSW-specific app use cases;
- small-group meetup discovery;
- first-time attendee connection flows;
- host-created meetup support;
- post-session continuation circles;
- product readiness milestones;
- QA and scale testing;
- privacy and data guardrails;
- boundaries with existing TCSW platforms;
- on-site or near-site support plan, if needed.

Operating question:

> **Can the app help a person at a large event find the people they came to meet?**

Product guardrail:

> Do not overpromise app scope before MVP readiness, user testing, privacy policy, and integration boundaries are clear.

## 8. Risks if team is under-resourced

| Risk | Cause | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Rachel becomes the bottleneck | Program production rests on one person | Burnout, missed details, weak room flow |
| Weldon becomes overloaded | Operations, venues, sponsors, and research all run through one person | Pipeline slows and decisions lack follow-through |

## 9. Team operating cadence

### 9.1 SMS core team meeting

Recommended cadence:

- weekly;
- Liban, Rachel, Weldon, Matthieu, Abhishek;
- focused on cross-functional blockers, decisions, deadlines, and dependencies.

Standing agenda:

1. What changed this week?
2. What decisions are blocked?
3. What does Angela / BETA need to approve?
4. What venue decisions affect programming?
5. What funding decisions affect staffing?
6. What creative assets are due?
7. What app milestones are due?
8. What risks need escalation?

### 9.2 Department check-ins

Each lead should maintain lightweight department tracking.

Suggested rhythm:

- Rachel: programming / production tracker;
- Weldon: venue and sponsor pipeline trackers;
- Matthieu: creative asset tracker;
- Abhishek: product milestone tracker;
- Liban: decision log and executive alignment tracker.

### 9.3 SMS x Angela operating meeting

Recommended cadence:

- weekly after authorization;
- focused on decisions, not status theater;
- outputs should update the Runtime OS.

Standing agenda:

1. decisions needed;
2. sponsor updates;
3. venue updates;
4. budget updates;
5. programming constraints;
6. public communication timing;
7. board / risk issues;
8. next 7-day actions.

## 10. Team decision-rights needs

The SMS team can only perform if responsibility and authority are aligned.

Needed from BETA:

- authority to build venue pipeline;
- authority to make venue recommendations;
- authority to draft campaign / landing page structure;
- authority to structure programming architecture;
- authority to define SMS app connection-layer use cases;
- authority to recommend sponsor packaging;
- authority to manage internal production trackers;
- clear approval process for public claims, sponsor promises, contracts, and budget commitments.

Principle:

> **SMS should own the recommendation and execution layer. BETA should retain final authority over legal, financial, institutional, and brand-risk decisions.**
