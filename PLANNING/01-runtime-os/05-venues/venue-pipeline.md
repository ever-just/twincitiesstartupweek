## 1. Purpose of this pipeline

This file converts venue strategy into venue action.

The venue pipeline exists to answer:

> **Which locations can actually hold TCSW 2026, who owns each relationship, what is the current status, what can each space support, what does it cost, and what is the next action?**

This is not simply a venue wish list.

It is the physical operating map for making TCSW 2026 believable, fundable, navigable, and deliverable.

---

## 2. Core venue pipeline thesis

TCSW 2026 cannot be programmed, sold, marketed, or experienced coherently until the physical system is legible.

The venue pipeline is the first major operating artifact that turns TCSW from an idea into a production reality.

The operating rule:

> **No room, no promise.**

Sponsor promises, programming promises, host expectations, attendee communication, SMS app use cases, and public campaign language must be grounded in confirmed or highly probable spaces.

---

## 3. Pipeline operating rule

Every venue record should answer ten questions:

1. **What is the venue?**
2. **Who owns the relationship?**
3. **Which dates are available?**
4. **What rooms / spaces are available?**
5. **What are the capacities?**
6. **What does it cost, and is in-kind possible?**
7. **What programming can it support?**
8. **What sponsor promises can it support?**
9. **What attendee experience risks exist?**
10. **What is the next action and due date?**

If those questions are unanswered, the venue is not ready to support external promises.

## 6. Venue scoring model

Use a 1–5 score for each factor.

| Factor | Question | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Capacity | Can the venue hold the expected audience and room needs? | High |
| Room mix | Does it have main room, breakouts, and gathering areas? | High |
| Availability | Is it available during Sept. 14–18 or relevant day(s)? | High |
| Cost | Is it affordable or in-kind? | High |
| Access | Can attendees enter, park, transit, and navigate easily? | High |
| Proximity | Is it near other useful venues, campuses, transit, food, or parking? | High |
| Sponsor fit | Can sponsors be visible without harming experience? | Medium / High |
| Programming fit | Can it support the intended day or track? | Medium / High |
| SMS app fit | Are there natural small-group meetup zones? | Medium / High |
| AV / tech | Can sessions run without expensive add-ons? | Medium |
| Hospitality | Can basic food, coffee, water, or gathering be supported? | Medium |
| Brand fit | Does it feel credible for Minnesota innovation? | Medium |
| Risk | What operational, cost, access, or trust risks exist? Lower is better. | High |

Suggested formula:

```text
Venue priority score = capacity + room_mix + availability + cost + access + proximity + sponsor_fit + programming_fit + app_fit + av + hospitality + brand_fit - risk
```

This score is a guide, not a substitute for judgment.

## 11. Outreach questions

Use these in every venue call.

### Availability

1. Are you available during Sept. 14–18, 2026?
2. Which dates and times are available?
3. Are there conflicts with classes, events, campus operations, or existing holds?

### Space inventory

4. What rooms are available?
5. What are the capacities by room?
6. What layouts are possible?
7. Is there a main gathering space?
8. Are there breakout rooms?
9. Are there informal meetup areas?
10. Is there outdoor or overflow space?

### Cost / in-kind

11. What is the rental cost?
12. Are deposits required?
13. Can any space be donated or discounted in-kind?
14. What services are included?
15. What services cost extra?

### Access

16. How do attendees enter?
17. Is check-in possible near the entrance?
18. Is the venue ADA accessible?
19. What are parking and transit options?
20. Are there security or ID requirements?

### AV / tech

21. What AV is included?
22. Are projectors, microphones, screens, and Wi-Fi included?
23. Is tech staff required?
24. Are outside AV vendors allowed?

### Food / hospitality

25. Can outside food be brought in?
26. Is catering required through the venue?
27. Are coffee / water / breakfast options available?
28. Are there restrictions on alcohol or evening events?

### Sponsor / signage

29. Can sponsor signage be placed on site?
30. Are there restrictions on logos, banners, tables, or activations?
31. Can sponsors host small gatherings in the space?

### Risk / logistics

32. Is insurance required?
33. Are permits required?
34. What staffing does the venue require?
35. What is the cancellation policy?
36. What deadlines apply?

## 15. Venue promise guardrails

Do not promise:

- specific rooms before confirmation;
- sponsor activations before venue approval;
- attendance capacity before capacity is verified;
- food / hospitality before venue rules are clear;
- app meetup zones before layout is understood;
- signage before venue restrictions are known;
- shuttle / transport support before funding and liability are clear;
- multi-location flow before maps and staffing exist.

Every venue-related promise must pass:

1. venue confirmation;
2. BETA approval;
3. production feasibility review;
4. budget check;
5. attendee experience review.
