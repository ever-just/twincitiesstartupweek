## 1. Core sponsor thesis

Sponsors are not being asked to fund a generic event.

They are being invited to help rebuild Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

The sponsor offer should be built around this sentence:

> **This is the year TCSW is being rebuilt as Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point. Your organization should be visibly part of bringing the ecosystem together again.**

The public emotional frame is:

> **The Great Reunion — the act of coming together again.**

The sponsor product must translate that frame into concrete value:

- civic role;
- visibility;
- access;
- talent connection;
- founder connection;
- industry relevance;
- community trust;
- measurable participation;
- association with the comeback year.

---

## 2. What the sponsor is actually buying

### 2.1 A role in rebuilding trust

Sponsors are buying visible association with the year TCSW is rebuilt with more discipline, coherence, and care.

This is especially important for past sponsors who may need to see that BETA is returning with a clearer operating structure.

### 2.2 A role in convening Minnesota's builders

Sponsors are buying the chance to help founders, funders, universities, students, operators, technologists, and institutions come back into relationship.

This is the civic value of TCSW.

### 2.3 A role in the AI / talent / innovation moment

Sponsors are buying relevance at a moment when AI, talent, technical fluency, and corporate innovation are converging.

This is not only a startup moment. It is a workforce, industry, and institutional adaptation moment.

### 2.4 A better-designed attendee experience

Sponsors are buying into a redesigned event experience where the goal is not only attendance, but meaningful connection.

SMS's role is to help make the week feel coherent, human, and shareable.

### 2.5 A visible community contribution

Sponsors should be able to tell their own stakeholders:

> **We helped make it possible for Minnesota's startup and technology ecosystem to come together again.**

---

## 3. Sponsor offer principles

### 3.1 Role-based, not only tier-based

Traditional Bronze / Silver / Gold tiers can still exist internally, but the external offer should emphasize sponsor roles.

A role gives the sponsor a story.

A tier gives the sponsor a receipt.

### 3.2 Every benefit must be feasible

Do not promise what the event cannot deliver.

Sponsor benefits must be tied to:

- confirmed venues;
- actual room capacity;
- confirmed programming;
- staffing capacity;
- marketing capacity;
- app readiness;
- registration / attendee data policy;
- BETA approval.

### 3.3 BETA remains the steward

Sponsors should understand that BETA remains the institutional home and nonprofit steward of TCSW.

SMS is the production and community experience partner.

### 3.4 Sponsor value must protect attendee trust

Sponsor activations should not make TCSW feel extractive, sales-heavy, or pay-to-play in a way that damages attendee trust.

The best sponsor role is to create real value for the room.

### 3.5 In-kind value should be packaged

Some partners may contribute more through venue, food, technology, media, printing, AV, or community reach than through cash.

Those contributions should be tracked, valued, recognized, and integrated into the sponsor architecture.

## 8. Sponsor promise approval checklist

Before any sponsor benefit is promised, answer:

1. Is the venue confirmed?
2. Is the room / space confirmed?
3. Is the staff capacity confirmed?
4. Is the sponsor role aligned with attendee value?
5. Is BETA comfortable with the brand association?
6. Does the benefit require legal or contract review?
7. Does the benefit depend on app readiness?
8. Does the benefit require attendee data?
9. Does the benefit require additional budget?
10. Can this promise be delivered even if attendance is lower than hoped?
11. Is the promise written clearly enough to avoid misunderstanding?
12. Who owns delivery?

If the answer is unclear, do not promise it yet.
