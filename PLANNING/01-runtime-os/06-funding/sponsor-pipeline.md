## 1. Purpose of this pipeline

This file converts the funding thesis into an operating system for sponsor action.

The pipeline exists to answer:

> **Who should be approached, why should they care, who owns the relationship, what role are they being invited into, and what is the next action?**

This is not just a list of companies.

It is the relationship infrastructure for funding **The Great Reunion**.

---

## 2. Core pipeline thesis

TCSW 2026 will not be funded because it needs to exist.

It will be funded when the right organizations believe:

> **This is the comeback year, and our organization should be visibly associated with bringing Minnesota's startup and technology ecosystem together again.**

Therefore, sponsor outreach must be:

- historically informed;
- relationship-led;
- role-based;
- time-sensitive;
- operationally grounded;
- board-safe;
- tied to real sponsor value;
- disciplined against overpromising.

---

## 3. Pipeline operating rule

Every sponsor record should answer seven questions:

1. **Why this sponsor?**
2. **What is their historical or strategic connection to TCSW / BETA / SMS / Minnesota innovation?**
3. **Who owns the relationship?**
4. **What sponsor role are we offering them?**
5. **What is the cash and/or in-kind target?**
6. **What is the next action?**
7. **What risk, sensitivity, or trust issue must be managed?**

If those questions are unanswered, the sponsor is not ready for outreach.

## 5. Sponsor priority scoring

Use a 1–5 score for each category.

| Factor | Question | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Historical relationship | Have they sponsored / hosted / participated before? | 1–5 |
| Strategic fit | Do they care about AI, talent, founders, capital, civic innovation, or Minnesota ecosystem visibility? | 1–5 |
| Relationship warmth | Do Angela, BETA, board, SMS, Weldon, Rachel, or Liban have a credible path in? | 1–5 |
| Funding capacity | Can they plausibly contribute meaningful cash or high-value in-kind support? | 1–5 |
| Timing urgency | Is this a moment where TCSW is especially relevant to them? | 1–5 |
| Trust sensitivity | Do they require repair, reassurance, or careful handling? Lower is better. | 1–5 |

Suggested formula:

```text
Priority score = historical + strategic + warmth + capacity + urgency - trust_sensitivity
```

This is a guide, not a substitute for judgment.

Criteria:

- warm relationship exists;
- sponsor has high strategic fit;
- sponsor can contribute cash or high-value in-kind;
- sponsor role is clear;
- Angela can credibly close or open conversation quickly.

Action:

> Prepare a tailored sponsor ask and schedule conversation within 7 days.

### Lane B — Relationship mapping needed

Criteria:

- sponsor is attractive;
- path in is unclear;
- board / SMS / Angela connection may exist but must be mapped.

Action:

> Identify warm intro path before outreach.

### Lane C — Research / validation needed

Criteria:

- sponsor appears relevant;
- historical involvement or current fit is unclear;
- sponsor should not be contacted until role and rationale are stronger.

Action:

> Validate through Weldon's research and relationship mapping.

## 10. Sponsor outreach sequence

### Step 1 — Validate history

Use Weldon's research package:

```text
00-weldon-research/data/sponsors/sponsors-database.md

00-weldon-research/data/sponsors/2026-sponsors.md

```

Answer:

- Did they sponsor before?
- What year(s)?
- What tier?
- What was their role?
- Did they receive enough value?
- Is there known trust sensitivity?

### Step 2 — Map relationships

Identify:

- Angela relationship;
- BETA board relationship;
- Liban / SMS relationship;
- Weldon research or contact path;
- Rachel program/community relationship;
- ecosystem warm intro;
- past TCSW contact.

### Step 3 — Assign sponsor role

Use `sponsor-offer.md` to assign a role:

- Founding Rebuild Partner;
- Great Reunion Partner;
- Industry Convener;
- AI + Innovation Partner;
- Founder Access Partner;
- Investor Day Partner;
- Student + Talent Partner;
- Community Connection Partner;
- Venue / Hospitality Partner;
- App-Enabled Connection Partner;
- Give Back / Hack Partner;
- Media / Storytelling Partner.

### Step 4 — Draft sponsor-specific rationale

One paragraph:

> Why this sponsor, why now, why TCSW, why this role, and what their support makes possible.

### Step 5 — Outreach

Angela closes cash sponsors unless otherwise agreed.

SMS may support relationship sourcing, sponsor narrative, materials, and follow-up.

### Step 6 — Feasibility review

Before any benefit is promised, run the sponsor promise checklist in `sponsor-offer.md`.

### Step 7 — Proposal / close

BETA controls final sponsor contract, invoice, and legal commitments unless otherwise agreed.

## 16. Sponsor promise guardrails

Do not promise:

- guaranteed attendance;
- guaranteed founder leads;
- guaranteed investor access;
- attendee data access;
- specific rooms before venue confirmation;
- exclusive category rights without BETA approval;
- app features before MVP readiness;
- media reach without a media plan;
- sponsor-hosted activations without staffing and space;
- anything that makes TCSW feel extractive.

Every sponsor promise must pass:

1. BETA approval;
2. production feasibility review;
3. attendee-value review;
4. venue / staffing / budget check.
