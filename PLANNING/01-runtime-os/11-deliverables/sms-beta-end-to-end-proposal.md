## 1. Executive summary

SMS proposes to serve as the **End-to-End Production & Community Experience Partner** for Twin Cities Startup Week 2026.

This proposal is not a request to take TCSW from BETA.

It is a proposed operating structure that allows BETA to remain the institutional steward while SMS carries the integrated production layer required to make the event believable, fundable, and deliverable.

The core division is:

> **BETA holds the institutional trust. Angela closes and stewards relationships. SMS produces the coherent public experience.**

TCSW 2026 is not simply a week of events.

It is the rebuild of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.

For that rebuild to succeed, every public touchpoint must increase perceived and actual value for BETA, sponsors, attendees, hosts, volunteers, partners, founders, investors, universities, students, and the broader ecosystem.

SMS believes the only responsible way to carry that challenge is through an end-to-end production and community experience model with clear authority, budget, compensation, decision rights, and risk boundaries.

---

## 2. Why this proposal exists

The Jun 3 discovery conversation made the challenge clear.

TCSW 2026 is operating under several conditions at once:

- BETA has deep ecosystem equity but limited operating capacity.
- Angela is carrying fundraising, marketing, sponsor repair, venue strategy, board communication, and event structure at the same time.
- BETA's small staff and accelerator work cannot be overloaded by TCSW production.
- TCSW has trust to rebuild from past years of operational strain and mismatch between promise and delivery.
- Funding is urgent, but funding depends on belief, perceived value, and operational credibility.
- Venue architecture is the immediate blocker because programming, sponsor promises, attendee flow, and app-enabled connection all depend on confirmed space.
- The event must be community-powered without becoming fragmented or chaotic.

The central risk is fragmentation.

Fragmented venues, fragmented programming, fragmented marketing, fragmented sponsor promises, fragmented ownership, and fragmented attendee experience will recreate the trust problem BETA is trying to repair.

Therefore:

> **End-to-end responsibility requires end-to-end authority.**

SMS is proposing an integrated production model because the challenge itself is integrated.

## 5. Scope of work

## 5.1 Venue and location architecture

SMS will support the sourcing, evaluation, and recommendation of the venue model for TCSW 2026.

Responsibilities:

- build and maintain venue pipeline;
- evaluate universities, stadium/event spaces, coworking spaces, civic venues, breweries, creative spaces, and partner venues;
- assess room capacity, cost, availability, access, AV, hospitality, sponsor fit, and attendee flow;
- identify anchor venue and nearby satellite options;
- produce venue scorecards;
- recommend location architecture;
- map rooms and day-by-day venue needs;
- flag venue constraints before sponsor or programming promises are made.

Operating rule:

> **No room, no promise.**

## 5.2 Programming architecture

SMS will develop the programming architecture for the week, not simply collect sessions.

Responsibilities:

- define day-by-day program logic;
- support host and speaker intake design;
- establish session quality standards;
- build run-of-show templates;
- align program structure with venue capacity;
- support anchor programming decisions;
- reduce conflicts and attendee confusion;
- design first-time attendee entry points;
- support community-powered programming without letting it become chaotic.

## 5.3 Brand, campaign, and marketing assets

SMS will help reset the public perception of TCSW 2026.

Responsibilities:

- campaign language;
- landing page structure;
- sponsor-facing assets;
- host-facing assets;
- attendee communications;
- social graphics;
- maps and wayfinding;
- signage system;
- visual and verbal consistency across the public journey.

The goal is not decoration.

The goal is to make TCSW feel credible, valuable, and shareable before September.

## 5.4 Sponsor support

Angela remains the sponsor closer.

SMS supports sponsor development by making the sponsor offer more compelling and operationally grounded.

Responsibilities:

- sponsor target intelligence;
- sponsor role recommendations;
- sponsor narrative;
- sponsor one-pager / menu support;
- sponsor pipeline maintenance, if authorized;
- sponsor activation feasibility review;
- sponsor experience design;
- alignment between sponsor promises and venue / programming reality.

SMS should not make final financial commitments or sponsor promises without BETA approval.

## 5.5 SMS app connection layer

The SMS app is positioned as the community connection layer for TCSW, not as a replacement for official ticketing or schedule infrastructure.

Responsibilities:

- define TCSW-specific app use cases;
- support small-group meetup discovery;
- support first-time attendee connection flows;
- enable post-session continuation circles;
- support host-created meetup moments;
- support sponsor-supported connection moments, if appropriate;
- define product readiness milestones;
- manage privacy, user experience, and platform-boundary guardrails.

Product thesis:

> **The SMS app helps large gatherings feel human.**

It should define:

- contingency.

Budget thesis:

SMS compensation should include:

- activation fee to begin formal work;
- monthly production retainer through September;
- department lead compensation;
- contractor / staffing budget;
- creative production support;
- product / engineering support;
- reimbursable expenses;
- any sponsor-sourced upside, if agreed.

SMS should not absorb BETA's organizational risk through unpaid labor without clear compensation, authority, and risk boundaries.

### 8.2 Funding thresholds

The proposal should use thresholds:

| Threshold | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Discovery | Build proposal, budget, venue pipeline, sponsor offer |
| Activation | Enough cash to begin formal SMS production work |
| Venue confidence | Enough cash or in-kind support to secure venue model |
| Minimum viable event | Enough to deliver a simple, credible TCSW |
| Target event | Enough to deliver a professionally rebuilt TCSW |
| Stretch event | Enough to add visibility and experience enhancements without creating chaos |

The sponsor strategy should shift from generic logo placement to role-based sponsorship.

Sponsor thesis:

> **TCSW's sponsor offer is not a plea for support. It is an invitation to take a visible role in the comeback year of Minnesota's startup and technology gathering point.**

Working sponsor roles include:

- Founding Rebuild Partner;
- Great Reunion Partner;
- Industry Convener;
- AI + Innovation Partner;
- Founder Access Partner;
- Investor Day Partner;
- Student + Talent Partner;
- Community Connection Partner;
- Venue / Hospitality Partner;
- App-Enabled Connection Partner;
- Give Back / Hack Partner;
- Media / Storytelling Partner.

Sponsor promise guardrail:

> **Every sponsor promise must be tied to operational reality.**

Do not promise attendance, rooms, investor access, app features, category exclusivity, or media reach unless those promises are supported by budget, venue, staffing, product readiness, and BETA approval.

## 10. Venue strategy

Venue strategy is the first major trust decision.

Recommended working direction:

> **Anchor hub + nearby satellites, with a bias toward a compact district.**

The venue model should prioritize:

- legibility;
- concentration;
- sponsor confidence;
- attendee ease;
- room capacity;
- app-friendly meetup zones;
- simple check-in;
- clear maps;
- venue captains;
- minimized transportation complexity.

Avoid two extremes:

1. an over-centralized venue that breaks the budget;
2. an over-distributed model that breaks attendee trust.
