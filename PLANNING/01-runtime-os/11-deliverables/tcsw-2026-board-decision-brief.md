# Board Decision Brief — TCSW 2026

**From:** Strangers Meeting Strangers (***SMS***) · **To:** the BETA Board · **Date:** June 9, 2026 · **Decision target:** June 19, 2026

**The finding.** ***SMS***'s internal investigation diagnoses TCSW's challenge as structural, not event production: the engine that should convert TCSW's ecosystem position into revenue, trust, and participation is under-built. ***SMS*** cannot in good faith accept production accountability without disclosing that diagnosis first.

**The opportunity.** BETA holds the official calendar for the one week Minnesota's startup attention concentrates. Run as three products — an **Open Doors Calendar** where hosts pay for official placement (highest margin, earliest revenue), a **sponsor/status product**, and a **ticket/access product** — the calendar becomes a revenue engine, not a brochure.

**The financial model (Proposal §§8–10).** Transaction fees and a board-approved, not-to-exceed production budget come off the top; ***SMS*** and BETA split only **Net New TCSW Revenue**. ***SMS***'s share is highest in the earliest, riskiest tranches and converges to BETA as the engine scales — blended 60/40 at $50k, 42/58 at $1M. BETA signs vendor contracts and owns the production budget; ***SMS*** does not fund event hard costs. Activation is **$30,000** — paid in cash, or recovered from the first net revenue if cash is unavailable.

**The decision requested (Proposal §16).**

1. authorize ***SMS*** as Revenue Engine Build, End-to-End Production, and Community Experience Partner;
2. approve the binding **Produced by SMS** public accreditation;
3. approve the $30,000 activation structure;
4. approve the net-revenue waterfall and event-cost responsibility model in principle;
5. confirm the signoff path — operating agreement executed by **June 19, 2026**; Sprint Day 1 is the first business day after written authorization.

**What BETA retains.** Brand ownership, board governance, sponsor contracts, budget approval above agreed thresholds, increasing revenue share as the engine scales — and the rebuilt engine itself. The waterfall applies to 2026 revenue alone; the products, playbook, and restored calendar value keep producing for BETA in 2027 and every year after. ***SMS*** is paid once, on 2026 performance.

> **Costs and fees come off the top; *SMS* and BETA split what the engine actually nets.**
