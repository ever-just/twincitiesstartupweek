# BETA's Directly Owned & Produced Events During TCSW
*Research Completed: June 2026*
*Sources: sessions-2018.csv (sched.com), sessions-2023.csv (sched.com), year summaries 2014–2025, recurring-events.md*

---

## The Core Finding

TCSW is an **open-submission unconference platform** — any organization can submit a session and it appears on the official schedule. Of 200+ events per year, **BETA directly produces only a small handful**. The rest are submitted and run by external orgs, sponsors, co-working spaces, VC firms, nonprofits, law firms, and community members.

The sched.com data makes this visible: each session lists a `(Host)` field. Events hosted by external organizations (MANOVA, MN Cup, Minnestar, TECHdotMN, GoKart Labs, BITCon, etc.) are clearly not BETA's productions — even though they appear on the TCSW schedule.

## Summary: BETA's True Production Footprint

Out of 200+ events on any given TCSW schedule, **BETA directly produces approximately 5–8 events**:

| Event | Every Year? | Core BETA Program? |
|---|---|---|
| BETA Showcase | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — oldest and most iconic |
| BETA Backers | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — invite-only investor matching |
| Opening Night Party | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — BETA runs it, sponsors brand it |
| Minnesota Startup Awards + Closing Party | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes — BETA staff are named hosts (confirmed 2023 sched) |
| Women's Pitch Fest | ✅ Most years | ✅ Yes — BETA-run competition |
| Optum Cup competition structure | 2023 only | ✅ Partially — BETA ran it, Optum named it |
| Walk and Talk | 2023+ | ⚠️ Community-organized, BETA facilitated |
| Innovation Crawl | 2025 | ⚠️ Co-produced with Naturally MN |

**The honest picture:** BETA is a **platform operator and festival director**, not a content producer at scale. Their job is to recruit 200+ sessions from the community, manage the sched.com schedule, coordinate venues, sell sponsorships, and produce the ~6 anchor events they directly own. That is their actual operational scope.
